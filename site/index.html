    Privacy by architecture

    Private by design, not by promise

    Cardugo can't leak what it never collects. There is no network code, no analytics, and no account. The only permission it ever requests is Face ID — used solely to unlock your encrypted vault on your device.

    No permissions requested

    No camera, photos, contacts, or location. The only system prompt you may ever see is Face ID — and only if you choose to enable it.

    No network access

    No URLSession, no web views, no backend. The app has no networking code, so it physically cannot send your data anywhere.

    No trackers or analytics

    Zero third-party SDKs. No analytics, no crash reporters, no ad networks. Nothing watches what you do.

    Face ID, on-device only

    Cards are encrypted with AES-256 and unlocked only by your PIN or Face ID. Your biometrics never leave the device.

    The card wallet that can't track you

    Privacy here isn't a setting you trust — it's enforced by how the app is built. Every detection, validation, and suggestion runs entirely from data bundled inside the app.

    Under the hood

    Security architecture

    Authentication

    • 6-digit PIN required on every launch
    • PBKDF2-SHA256 · 200,000 iterations
    • Progressive lockout after repeated failures
    • Optional Face ID / Touch ID / Optic ID
    • 15-minute reveal session window

    Encryption

    • AES-256-GCM on every sensitive field
    • Unique random nonce per encryption
    • Data key wrapped by your PIN
    • Biometric key sealed in the iOS Keychain
    • Zero third-party libraries

    You stay in control

    Backup & restore

    Encrypted .cardugo file

    Export an AES-256 encrypted backup protected by a password you choose — separate from your app PIN. Share it via Files, AirDrop, or any cloud you trust.

    Non-destructive import

    Importing merges cards into your existing collection without wiping anything. All decryption happens on-device, with no server involved.
